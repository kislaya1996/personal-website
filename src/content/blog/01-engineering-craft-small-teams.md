Craft in a small team is less about ceremony and more about leaving the work easier to change than when you found it.

The practical version is simple: name tradeoffs, keep interfaces boring, write down the reasoning that will disappear from memory, and make the next debugging session shorter.

## What matters most

- Keep modules understandable before making them clever.
- Prefer small reversible decisions while the product is still moving.
- Write notes where future maintainers will actually find them.

> Good engineering taste often shows up as restraint: fewer moving parts, clearer boundaries, and just enough documentation.
